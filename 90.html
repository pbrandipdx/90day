MATCH STRATEGY TO SITUATION

No "One Size Fits All." Diagnose your situation using the STARS model:

Start-Up

Building strategy, structures, systems from scratch. Assembling capabilities.

Turnaround

Saving a business in serious trouble. Making tough calls under pressure.

Accelerated Growth

Managing rapid expansion. Scaling systems and integrating new talent.

Realignment

Reenergizing a previously successful org. Convincing others change is needed.

Sustaining Success

Preserving vitality and taking it to the next level. Playing good defense.

Each situation requires a different focus (e.g., Turnarounds need quick technical learning & bold moves; Realignments need cultural/political learning & deliberate moves).

NEGOTIATE SUCCESS

Have 5 key conversations with your leader to align and set expectations:

  1. Situational Diagnosis: Do you both see the STARS situation the same way? What's their role?
  2. Expectations: What does success look like? How will performance be measured? Understand their priorities. Underpromise, overdeliver.
  3. Resources: What do you need (money, people, authority, backing) for your specific STARS situation?
  4. Style: Understand your boss‚Äôs communication, learning, and decision-making style. Adapt.
  5. Personal Development: Ask for feedback on your hard and soft skills.

SECURE EARLY WINS

"Early wins excite and energize people and build your personal credibility. Done well, they help you create value for your new organization earlier and reach the break-even point much more quickly."

This isn't always about shipping features. It can be mitigating risks, driving clarity, getting commitments, or defining a go-to-market plan. Preventing problems is often easier than fixing them.
